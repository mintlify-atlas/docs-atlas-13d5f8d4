# Research Abstract

## Purpose and Objectives

The purpose of this research is to accurately make predictions on student performance to tailor education to individual ability levels. By analyzing large-scale educational log data from Riiid, an AI EdTech company based in South Korea, we aim to enable higher efficiency in education that improves student outcomes and opens doors for social mobility.

<Note>
  Meeting students with support for exactly where they're struggling is an efficient use of both the student and educators' time. This research is important to educators, students, parents, and nearly everyone with a stake in education.
</Note>

## Approach

Two complementary machine learning techniques were implemented:

### 1. K-Means Clustering

<Steps>
  <Step title="Feature Selection">
    Selected key features: mean accuracy, standard deviation in performance, and number of attempts
  </Step>
  <Step title="Clustering">
    Applied K-Means algorithm to identify distinct performance groups
  </Step>
  <Step title="Validation">
    Used elbow method to determine optimal number of clusters
  </Step>
  <Step title="Analysis">
    Revealed performance groups by capturing variation in accuracy, consistency, and engagement
  </Step>
</Steps>

The K-Means clustering successfully identified distinct student groups based on:
- **Accuracy** - Overall performance on questions
- **Standard Deviation** - Consistency in performance
- **Number of Attempts** - Engagement and persistence patterns

### 2. Regularized Least Squares Model

<Steps>
  <Step title="Feature Engineering">
    Created comprehensive features including mean accuracy, consistency trends, performance trajectory, and tutorial effectiveness
  </Step>
  <Step title="Model Training">
    Trained regularized least squares model with λ=0.01 to prevent overfitting
  </Step>
  <Step title="Evaluation">
    Assessed model performance using accuracy and precision metrics
  </Step>
</Steps>

Feature engineering was critical to model success, incorporating metrics such as:
- Mean accuracy and consistency trends
- Recent performance vs. historical performance
- Time between attempts and time-accuracy correlation
- Tutorial explanation effectiveness
- Performance trend over time

### Model Performance

The regularized least squares model with **λ=0.01** achieved:
- **~80.7% accuracy** in predicting whether a student will perform above or below the median
- **100% precision** in predicting student success (no false positives)

These metrics demonstrate that the model can reliably identify students who need support without incorrectly flagging successful students.

### Clustering Insights

The K-Means clustering revealed distinct performance groups, enabling:
- Identification of students with consistent high performance
- Detection of struggling students with low accuracy and high variability
- Recognition of engaged students (high attempt counts) who may need different support
- Understanding of consistency patterns that indicate stable vs. unstable performance

## Implications

<Note>
  These findings suggest that **predictive modeling with performance-based clustering** can support the development of personalized education strategies, since educators can reliably determine whether a student needs help or falls within a performance group that is classified as intervention needing.
</Note>

### Educational Impact

1. **Early Intervention** - Identify struggling students before they fall too far behind
2. **Resource Optimization** - Allocate educator time and support to students who need it most
3. **Personalized Learning** - Tailor educational content and pacing to student performance groups
4. **Outcome Prediction** - Anticipate student success and adjust strategies accordingly
