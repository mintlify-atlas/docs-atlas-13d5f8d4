## Confusion Matrix Breakdown

The model's performance is evaluated through a confusion matrix that compares predicted outcomes against actual student performance:

<Info>
  The model classifies students as either needing intervention (positive) or performing adequately (negative) based on their mean accuracy relative to the median.
</Info>

### Key Metrics

- **True Positives (TP)**: Students correctly identified as needing intervention
- **True Negatives (TN)**: Students correctly identified as performing adequately
- **False Positives (FP)**: Students incorrectly flagged for intervention
- **False Negatives (FN)**: Students who need help but were not identified

<Note>
  The 100% precision indicates that when the model predicts a student needs intervention, it is always correct. This is critical for efficient resource allocation in educational settings.
</Note>

## K-Means Clustering Results

### Cluster Configuration

<Accordion title="Clustering Parameters">
  - **Number of Clusters (K)**: 20
  - **Max Iterations**: 20
  - **Features Used**: Mean accuracy, standard deviation of accuracy, number of attempts, average time, mean explanation effectiveness
</Accordion>

The K-Means clustering analysis successfully revealed distinct performance groups by capturing variation across three primary dimensions:

1. **Mean Accuracy**: Overall correctness rate
2. **Standard Deviation of Accuracy**: Consistency in performance
3. **Number of Attempts**: Student engagement level

### Performance Groups Identified

The clustering algorithm identified multiple performance groups characterized by:

- Students with high accuracy and low variability (consistent high performers)
- Students with moderate accuracy and high engagement (improving learners)
- Students with low accuracy despite multiple attempts (need intervention)
- Students with inconsistent performance (need targeted support)

<Note>
  The choice of K=20 clusters was validated using the elbow method to balance granularity with interpretability.
</Note>

## Regularization Parameter

The regularized least squares model uses λ=0.01 to prevent overfitting:

```julia
β = regularized_ls(X_train, y_train, .01)
```

<Info>
  Regularization helps the model generalize better to unseen students by penalizing overly complex coefficient patterns.
</Info>

## Model Validation

The model was evaluated using an 80/20 train-test split with random shuffling to ensure unbiased assessment:

- **Training Set**: 80% of student data for parameter estimation
- **Test Set**: 20% of student data for performance evaluation
- **Evaluation Method**: Binary classification with 0.5 threshold

<Note>
  All features were normalized using z-score standardization to ensure equal contribution across different scales.
</Note>
